Classic Cheesecake

By: Henrik

This classic cheesecake with a creamy and smooth texture is topped with a sweet and tangy raspberry sauce, making it a perfect dessert for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 250g digestive biscuits
  • 125g unsalted butter, melted
  • 800g cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 200g caster sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 200ml sour cream
  • 1 lemon, zest only

For topping:

  • 300g raspberries
  • 3 tbsp caster sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C/140°C fan/gas mark 3. Grease a 20cm springform cake tin.
  2. Crush the digestive biscuits in a food processor or put them in a plastic bag and bash with a rolling pin until they resemble fine crumbs. Mix the melted butter into the biscuit crumbs and press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared tin. Chill in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and caster sugar together until smooth and creamy. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract, sour cream and lemon zest.
  4. Pour the mixture into the tin and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake for 50-60 minutes or until set but with a slight wobble in the centre.
  5. Turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake to cool inside for 1 hour, then remove and leave to cool completely at room temperature.
  6. For the topping, place the raspberries and caster sugar in a small saucepan and heat gently until the raspberries break down and release their juices. Remove from the heat and push the mixture through a sieve to remove any seeds.
  7. Chill the cheesecake in the fridge for at least 2 hours or overnight. Serve topped with the raspberry sauce.

Enjoy your delicious cheesecake!
