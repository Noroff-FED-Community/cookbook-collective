Semi-Sourdough Bagels

By: Lily

Looking for another way to use up the sourdough starter that's floating around your fridge? These bagels are a flavourful option, that can easily be frozen for a quick meal on the go.

Here's what you need:

  • 6 grams dry yeast
  • 10 grams sugar
  • 15 grams malt powder
  • 185 grams sourdough starter
  • 350 grams sifted wheat flour
  • 90 grams sifted rye flour
  • 6 grams salt
  • 20 grams of either honey, dark brown sugar, molasses, or malt extract
  • Flake salt or toppings of your choice

Directions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, stir together yeast, sugar and malt powder with 120 grams of body temperature water. Allow to rest for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the sourdough starter and 150 grams of water, mixing thoroughly.
  3. Last, add the wheat flour, rye flour, and salt to the bowl with the starter. Combine until a shaggy dough forms.
  4. Turn the dough onto a well floured work surface and hand knead for 5-8 minutes, or until a smooth dough forms. Do NOT use a machine to knead the dough. Home use machines are generally not strong enough to mix bagel dough, and doing so could break the motor.
  5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover and allow to rise until almost doubled in size.
  6. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces (approximately 80 grams each). Roll each piece into a tight ball and allow to rest for another 10 minutes.
  7. Bring 1.5 litres of water to simmer in a wide pot, and stir in the 20 grams of sweetener of your choice.
  8. Preheat your oven to 210 degrees Celsius.
  9. Form dough balls into bagels, and rest another five minutes.
  10. Prepare any toppings or salt that you would like to flavour your bagels, and a baking sheet with parchment paper to bake your bagels.
  11. Working with 2-4 bagels at a time, gently place bagels in the pot and simmer for 2 minutes on each side. Be careful not to overcrowd the pot, so that they do not stick together. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on the baking sheet.
  12. While bagels are still sticky, sprinkle any salt or toppings you have chosen.
  13. Once all of your bagels are prepared, bake for 20 minutes or until very golden brown. Depending on how evenly your oven cooks, you may need to rotate the pan half way through cooking.
  14. Bagels are best enjoyed while freshly baked. If you do not plan to eat them within 24 hours, it's better to pre-slice and then freeze them. Frozen bagels can be reheated in the toaster, ready for easy breakfasts.
